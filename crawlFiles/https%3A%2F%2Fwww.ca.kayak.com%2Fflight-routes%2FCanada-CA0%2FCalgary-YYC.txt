Calgary International Airport (YYC) is the main airport in Calgary, located around 17 kilometres from the city center. Calgary International Airport serves as the hub for Air Canada, Lynx Air, and WestJet. Consider flying to Calgary with either of the three airlines to get many domestic and international connections from YYC. Lynx Air offers connections to many cities in Canada at an affordable price. WestJet is the main airline operating from Calgary International Airport. WestJet has flights to over 30 destinations year-round and offers seasonal flights to around 30 more destinations. When traveling on flights to Calgary and looking for an on-site airport hotel the choice of the Calgary Airport Marriott In-Terminal Hotel is clear. This hotel offers dramatic views of the Calgary skyline and the Rocky Mountains and is just a short walk from baggage claim in the terminal building. If you have landed after your flight to Calgary but do not know where to head to next, stop in at the InfoCentre located in the arrivals hall. If you have the need to store any of your luggage after your flight to Calgary, the airport offers a luggage storage service. This service is available 24 hours per day and can be found by visiting the InfoCenter service desk that is located in arrivals hall of Concourse B. If you are looking for a convenient and inexpensive way to get to downtown Calgary after your flight, Calgary Transit offers four different bus lines that run from the airport to various points in the Calgary area. Tickets for the bus can either be purchased at Mac‚Äôs Convenience Store or from the vending machines that are located at Bus Bay 7. Many people travel to Calgary as their gateway to Banff in the Canadian Rockies. A great way to make the trip from the airport to Banff is on the Banff Airporter. This scheduled bus service offers guaranteed departures that mean you can rest assured that, when your flight lands, you will be on your way to the mountains on time and at a price you can afford.
